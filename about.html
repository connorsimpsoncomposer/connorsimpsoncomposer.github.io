Connor Simpson is a composer based in State College, Pennsylvania. Connor’s work explores a wide variety of contemporary styles and aesthetics. His research includes traditional and modern methods of composition including serialism, abstract mathematics, and computer assisted composition. Connor has written works for renowned ensembles such as Loadbang and Hypercube. His works include Wrong Chain for solo violin, ill for quadraphonic fixed media, and Prancer for wind ensemble. Connor is pursuing his Bachelor of Music with Dr. Baljinder Sekhon and Dr. Sarah Genevieve Burghart Rice at Penn State University.

Connor's work has been selected for esteemed international music festivals. He has received premieres of his original works through the New York City Electroacoustic Music Festival, Longy’s Divergent Studio and the SoundSCAPE new music festival. Connor has been awarded the Burt L Fenner Scholarship in Music composition, the Valley Family Fund, and William J and Lois Kesterton Leight Memorial Fund. Connor is now serving as the PSU Living Music Secretary and served on the 2019 and 2020 Penn State Young Composers Contest panel and the 2021 Penn State New Music Festival.
